For Beginners

Why Do People Use Crypto?

A clear, honest explanation for anyone who has ever wondered what crypto actually is.

What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is digital money that exists only on the internet. Unlike the euro or dollar — which are controlled by governments and banks — crypto is managed by a global network of computers following shared rules.

Think of it like email versus postal mail. Email did not replace mail instantly — it took time, had new problems, but solved things mail could not. Crypto is doing something similar to money.

Why people use it
Global Transfers

Move money across borders

Sending money internationally can take days and cost significant fees. Crypto can move value across borders in minutes, for a fraction of the cost.

Financial Access

No bank required

1.4 billion adults worldwide have no access to a bank. Crypto gives anyone with a phone access to financial services.

Investment

Potential returns

Some people buy crypto hoping its value increases over time, similar to buying shares. This comes with significant risk of loss.

Fixed Supply

Inflation hedge

Bitcoin has a fixed supply of 21 million coins. Some use it to protect savings from inflation, since no one can print more.

Smart Contracts

Programmable money

Ethereum lets developers build financial apps — loans, insurance, savings — that run automatically without a bank controlling them.

Ownership

True digital ownership

Crypto enables genuine ownership of digital items — art, game assets, music rights — that cannot be deleted by any company.

The honest risks

Extreme Volatility

Bitcoin has dropped 80% from its peak. Twice. It has also risen 10,000%+. Be prepared for wild swings in both directions.

No Safety Net

If you lose your password (seed phrase), your crypto is gone forever. There is no forgot password button and no bank to call.

Scams Are Common

The space is full of scams — fake projects, pump-and-dump schemes, and phishing attacks. If it sounds too good to be true, it is.

Regulatory Risk

Governments worldwide are still writing rules around crypto. New regulations could significantly affect the value of your holdings.

Common myths, cleared up
Myth: Crypto is only used for crime
Fact: Less than 1% of crypto transactions are linked to illegal activity. Cash is far more widely used for crime.
Myth: Bitcoin is anonymous
Fact: Bitcoin transactions are public and permanent on the blockchain. It is actually easier to trace than cash.
Myth: It is too late to get into crypto
Fact: People have said this since Bitcoin was $1, $100, $1,000, and $10,000. Nobody knows the future.
Myth: All crypto is the same
Fact: There are thousands of coins with very different purposes — from digital gold to programmable platforms to tokens with no real utility.
